It was recognized among some ancient people that what makes a seed grow is equivalent to a command contained within in it. them in a way analogous to the way the Universe was created by "the Word" in the gospel of John. Hence, the symbolism of the seed repeatedly used by Jesus in the gospels such as in the parable of the sower where the seed sown is likened to his message of the kingdom of heaven. One implication of this is that the Universe is an expression of God's will, i.e. that it isn't just a work of art, but, like the Bible, contains a message, unadulterated and unmediated, and thus, when we walk through the world it could well be the case that God's parables are all araound us. But also, looking at the parables in the Bible, we could say that every human comes into this existence as a seed.

Now, some might say this is obvious, every human begins when a single-celled organism we call a sperm which is just another word for seed, and which contains within it a command which drives it to a destination with its other half. Within the womb of woman is the bridal chamber we call an egg where the chromosomes within the seed from her man meets its bride, the two come together as one, and a new life begins. But this life must be born again, once spoken by prophets, but the words spoken within the heart of every human, such that we might say every human comes into this world as a seed.
